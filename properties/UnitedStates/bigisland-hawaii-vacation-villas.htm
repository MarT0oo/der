About The Big Island

The Big Island is Just that, 4,028 square miles of Big Hawaii with over 185,000 people residing their annually.  Home to some of the most amazing sites, with one of the world’s most active volcano, which most people seem to find utterly fascinating.  This Island is also home to  Akaka Falls and some amazing natural wonders, which is why most people journey to this fantastic destination.  You can go from a big city with lots of shopping and dining to Wao Kele O Puna which is Hawaii’s largest low-lying rainforest remaining.  It truly is exploring the most beautiful parts of the world within one island.

Some things to explore while you are on the island are: Kua Bay Beach Park in  Kona, Makalawena Beach which is one of the more remote and private beaches also in Kona, Mauna Kea Beach which is lovely and has sandy white beaches, Punalu'u Beach Park which is an amazing Black Sand Beach and is somewhere you’ll want to go to watch the Sea Turtles and enjoy the fish pond in the park, Kahaluu is know as one of the best snorkeling sites, and Spencer Beach is wonderful for families looking for fun in a very lovely and protected area.  These are just some of the lovely places to visit on the big Island, for more please visit the local Hawaii Chamber of Commerce.

Because of its popularity there are wonderful villas, mansions, oceanfront homes, available for short term rentals. As well there are great places for weddings and honeymoons.
