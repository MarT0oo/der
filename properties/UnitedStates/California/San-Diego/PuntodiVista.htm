Punto di Vista, Loma Portal, San Diego, California Vacation Rental

 

Welcome to Punto di Vista! This unique 3 bd / 2.5 ba home provides all the comfort and amenities guest desire when visiting San Diego, CA. The home has multiple levels and panoramic views of the Pacific Ocean, Sea World, Mission Bay, and the hillside homes of La Jolla and Bay Park areas. This home is set up with indoor / outdoor living and is perfect for families looking to relax while in San Diego and still super close to all the attractions the city offers.

eJust to name a few of the nearby attractions: Sea World, San Diego Zoo, Balboa Park, Downtown San Diego Gaslamp District. Home is also in close proximity to tons of bars and restaurants of which many are located in Liberty Station. There you'll find establishments such as Breakfast Republic, Con Pane, Stone Brewery, Coldstone Ice Cream, and so much more. Property is also only a 2 min. drive to Einsteins Bagels and two Starbucks locations

Six amplified zones allow music to be localized or to be distributed throughout the entire home (decks/patios, inclusive). Internet Radio/Music, too, is provided by XM/Sirius and Slacker/LiveWire. Further, you can cast/mirror your device to any of our 4 TVs (in the kitchen and in each of our 3 bedrooms). Oh yea, the music in the Master Bedroom and in the Great Room is especially sweat, with Yamo speakers and 12" subwoofers.

Movies, anyone? More than 1400 movie titles are at your fingertips, literally. They can be viewed by scrolling through their electronic jackets on each of the bedroom TVs. The Master Bedroom, though, has 5.1 Surround Sound with subwoofer and shaker speakers embedded into the bed. Oh yea!

Our pool is 10' wide by 40' long. Except for the center of the pool, which is 5' deep, the rest of the pool is 4' deep. The pool is heated by solar from about mid-April through the end of October, and features a water-slide. Best of all, our pool is virtually chemical-free. We use the same type of copper-ionizer that was once (and maybe still is) used by NASA to purify water on the Space Station.
