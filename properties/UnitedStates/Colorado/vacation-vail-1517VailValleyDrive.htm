1517 Vail Valley Drive, Vail, Colorado Vacation Rental

 

This 3,653 square foot home has four bedrooms, four bathrooms, and can sleep up to 10 guests. Just 20 yards from the bus stop it is easy to get to and from the slopes and Vail Village when staying in this beautiful property.

Nestled in the Vail Golf Course Neighborhood, this 4 bedroom residence is only 1.4 miles from Vail Village. With plenty of space for gathering, the 10 guests that this home can accommodate will have plenty of space to relax and rest after a day exploring the mountain; be it winter or summer!

During the winter months the free Town of Vail Bus comes every 30 minutes and the stop is only yards from the home. A few minutes later guests find themselves at the base of the slopes in Golden Peak ready for a day on the mountain.

During the summer months the paved roads and bike paths make strolling or biking into town a breeze, and the Betty Ford Alpine Gardens are a great stopping point in between along with a massive children’s playground. Not to mention, residing in the Golf Course neighborhood guests can access the course with ease.

This home not only comes with 4 bedrooms and 4 bathrooms, but also has a large great room with floor to ceiling windows, two gathering areas, stone fireplace, and flat screen television. The great room blends seamlessly into the dining room and kitchen area so guests can comfortably gather and recap their day in the mountains. The home is also equipped with a gas grill, billiards table, heated garage with parking for one car, and Wi-Fi throughout.

To help ease those sore muscles after a day of activities we provide the guests that stay here complimentary passes to the Vail Vitality Center and Vail Athletic Club while in residence.

About The Area

Vail, Colorado, is a small town at the base of Vail Mountain, home of the massive Vail Ski Resort. Set within White River National Forest, the town is a gateway for winter sports such as skiing and snowboarding. It’s also a summertime destination for golfing, hiking and cultural festivals. Gore Creek, popular for fly fishing, runs directly through the town center.
