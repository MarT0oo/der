Private penthouse on 9th floor of The Ritz-Carlton, Bachelor Gulch, a central resort location with mountain views from every room.

Timber Lodge 9th floor, central resort location atop the award-winning Ritz-Carlton, Bachelor Gulch presents miles of ski mountain views from every room. For even more views, a large terrace spanning nearly the entire length of the 2,650 square foot residence can be accessed from all three bedroom suites—and from the entire living area—and looks out onto the core of the iconic ski slopes. Also in view is the après-ski activity below, including live music around the fire pit.

The living area is centered around a two-sided moss stone fireplace, whose cozy warmth can be enjoyed while you’re lounging in the sitting area or tucking into a delicious dinner at the 8-person dining table. Ralph Lauren, Baker, Milling Road and custom-made furnishings create a classic slope-side ambiance, as do the 9-foot wood coffer ceilings and hardwood floors. A gourmet kitchen and state-of-the-art entertainment system are just a few of this penthouse’s perks.

All of the amenities at Ritz-Carlton, Bachelor Gulch, are yours to enjoy when you stay here—including the prime ski-in / ski-out location, 21,000 ft² spa and fitness center, Ritz Kids, in-room dining, and all the other services expected from a Ritz-Carlton resort.

Master Bedroom Suite: A spacious master suite boasts a king-sized bed, a private balcony, a sitting area with a large-screen TV and walk-in closet. An en-suite bathroom includes a WC with bidet, plus a two-person Jacuzzi tub, steam shower and heated floor—all perfect for a post-ski defrosting.

Guest Suite 2: Here, find a queen-sized bed, a walk-out balcony, and an en-suite bathroom.

Guest Suite 3: This cozy room has two twin beds, a walk-out balcony, and an en-suite bathroom.

Fully Equipped Chef Style Kitchen: Granite counters, stainless steel appliances and cabinetry stocked with every culinary tool imaginable make for easy and intuitive cuisine creation in this gourmet kitchen. Plus, barstools sidled up to the granite island means the cook—or cooks—can mingle with everyone else while they work their magic.

Great Room & Living Area: This penthouse showcases a floor plan that’s just right for family get-togethers. The layout includes a large great room with a two-sided moss rock fireplace at the helm to separate the living and dining areas, plus a sofa and chair configuration to encourage conversation in view of both the mountains and the flickering fire.

About The Area

Home to the iconic Rocky Mountains, it’s no wonder that Colorado is the number one destination for ski trips in the United States. With 21 first-class ski resorts scattered throughout the Rockies, ski and snowboard enthusiasts travel far and wide each winter to experience the mountains firsthand. Colorado is truly a year-round destination, however, with renowned dining, shopping, and golfing bringing in tourists long after the ski season has ended.

Featuring several world-class ski resorts, such as Vail, Aspen and Beaver Creek, Colorado is firmly grounded as one of the most sought after luxury vacation spots for affluent travelers. Given a plethora of luxury mountain cottages, villas, chalets and rental homes, it’s no surprise Colorado is often host to celebrities and royalty.
