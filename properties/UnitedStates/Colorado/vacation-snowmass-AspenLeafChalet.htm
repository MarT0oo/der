Summary of location and ski access:

The most effortless Ski-Out access from the Chalet is to the Assay Hill ski slope (leads to Base Village, Treehouse Ski & Snowboard School, Village Express and Elk Camp Gondola etc), and it only takes a few minutes. Exit from the garage and ski down in the front yard to Wood Road. This 100 feet long designated ski path and ski bridge is fairly easy but not for complete beginners (see pictures). Cross Wood Road and either ski down along Village Run Circle or walk about 75 feet and then ski down about 200 feet to Assay Hill alongside a building.

You also have Ski-Out access to Adams Avenue ski slope (which leads to the Base Village) from the back of the house, which takes about 3-4 minutes. It is mostly easy skiing downhill, a little bit straight, and at the end a bit of upward hiking to get up on the slope. Suitable for all levels of skiers.

Ski-In access is from Adams Avenue above Edgewood Lane tunnel, along a marked path. The route involves some powder snow and traversing of trees, so it is not for beginners. If Edgewood Lane has snow this route takes about 1-2 minutes.

It is merely a 3 minute drive down to the Snowmass Village base camp where the Treehouse (kids ski school and camp), the Village Express and the Elk Camp Gondola are all located.

You can get around for $1 per person by calling “Dial-A-Ride”. This service will pick you up bring you anywhere within Snowmass, be it for skiing, restaurant visits or grocery shopping. Also, all buses between Snowmass and Aspen are free, and it will take you to all the other ski mountains, Buttermilk, the Highlands and Ajax.
