Four Pines 07, Teton Village, Wyoming Vacation Rental

 

A rustic elegance pervades this expansive residence, sited in the foothills of the Teton Range. True to its name, this Western refuge sits nestled within pines and aspens, within sight of the local landmark that inspired its name: the treed terrain of Four Pines, a beloved destination within Jackson Hole Mountain Resort.

Context aside, the house itself delights guests. The front porch, defined by a chinked enclave, greets guests graciously with a barnwood door. The entryway welcomes with an antique cabinet and studded leather bench. Just beyond is an antique table and rustic arched, panned mirror.

Guest bedroom one continues the subtle palette found throughout the home. A set of leather luggage racks, antler mount, and wooden paneled headboards adorn the two extra-long twin beds which can be converted into a king bed, as pictured. . The ensuite bath offers a tile shower and vanity. The second downstairs bedroom offers the same set-up as guest bedroom one with upholstered bedframes, plaid wool blankets, wooden benchand an ensuite bath. Guest bedroom three echoes the master with mountain vistas visible from the king bed. A colorful, chevron patterned blanket covers the bed and a pair of studded armchairs invite relaxation.

On the second floor landing, a table allows for a work interlude, while the pair of leather chairs induces lounging.

Truly open in concept and subtle in palette, the great room encompasses the kitchen, dining and living areas, and even draws in the drama outdoors through the picture windows that encircle the space. The living area centers around the stone fireplace, flanked by two sofas and a pair of armchairs. The dining table seats six in plum colored leather chairs. Overlooking the dining area, the kitchen caters to gourmet chefs with its tawny granite countertops and stainless steel appliances. Outside, a balcony with a table, enables meals with a view.

The master bedroom beckons with its gray wool duvet enshrouding the king bed, backed by a panel-tufted headboard. Textured armchair makes for a warm fireside moment. The ensuite bath offers a tub, glass shower, granite double vanity and panoramic views. While the abundant domestic diversions could keep guests busy inside, a Four Pines stay would be incomplete without venturing outside. A bike path borders the property, allowing for car-free travel between top spots including the southern entrance to Grand Teton National Park. Downtown Jackson is a short drive away, as is the hamlet of Wilson. Whether fly fishing or horseback riding, mountain biking or backcountry skiing, this Shooting Star retreat allows you to choose your own adventure.
