Granite Ridge Lodge 03, Teton, Wyoming Vacation Rental

 

The slopeside lifestyle, epitomized by this sumptuous three-story home, will spoil you. Summer finds you steps from miles upon miles of hiking and biking trails. Come winter, fresh powder awaits you off the back patio. Perched at the base of Jackson Hole Mountain Resort, Granite Ridge Lodge 3 offers matchless outdoor convenience. Not to mention nature immersion: Nestled in a glade of evergreens, valley and mountain views are visible from every room, and a rocky stream meanders through the property, adding a babbling soundtrack to your stay.

The foyer greets you on the mid-level, with chic details defining the experience from the get-go. A rope-slung metal mirror hangs above a barnwood console. Just beyond, the open-concept great room encompasses the kitchen, dining room and living area under a ceiling of exposed beams. A stone fireplace anchors the gracious space, with built-in storage flanking its rustic hearth and a prized landscape painting setting the aesthetic tone. A studded denim sofa and matching leather armchairs surround a weathered wood and metal coffee table, all atop a burgundy Oriental rug. The adjoining dining room seats eight comfortably in white studded armchairs beneath a candle chandelier. A high-top breakfast bar delineates the kitchen, a gourmet expanse marked by ebony marble countertops, burnished bronze accents and a wide island. Stainless steel appliances and an ample stove elevate the experience.

A polished wood staircase leads to the second floor, where a family area lives on the landing. The perfect perch for board-game playing, the loft offers a leather sofa, cowhide rug and woven poufs. An abstract bison painting lends liveliness.

The airy master bedroom, tucked on the top floor, features a spacious floor-plan beneath the peaked roof. A king bed, ensconced in a duvet, faces a rock fireplace, leather armchair, and a flat-screen TV on a rustic chest of drawers. The suite also includes a large walk-in closet and roomy ensuite bath, sheathed in creamy granite tile, with a soaking tub and glass shower. And the pièce de résistance: A private deck with a pair of Adirondack chairs facing the slopes.

The second bedroom is malleable with two twin beds that can combine to form a king. A private deck with two Adirondack chairs invites valley viewing. The adjacent bathroom pairs charcoal granite tile with stained wood cabinetry.

The third bedroom accommodates a king bed, wrapped in woolens, and two walls of windows. A leather and chrome accent chair and matching footstool make for a sleek reading nook. On the lower level, a den allows for unwinding with a plush leather couch, leather bean bag chairs, an expansive TV, and a woven pouf. In the corner, a dartboard invites competition. Down the hall, a fourth bedroom offers a queen bed, plush armchair and ensuite bath. The nearby mudroom provides a convenient connection between the house and attached two-car garage, with built-in cubbies, bench and coat rack.

In spite of all the indoor amenities, a flagstone patio—jutting off the back of the house—lures guests outside with a hot tub, dining table with six upholstered chairs, and a gas grill. Bordered by Grand Teton National Park, the Granite Ridge neighborhood is known for its luxurious accessibility, with the slopes constituting the backyard and the retail, culinary and nightlife diversions of Teton Village only a short walk away. Without a doubt, Granite Ridge is a formidable launchpad for outdoor adventurers.
