About Kauai

Kauai is about 550 square miles and is the oldest and  northernmost of the main Hawaiian Islands.  This Island is known for its beautiful sandy beaches and extraordinary  Waimea Canyon one of the Islands foremost natural wonders created from years of erosion that carves itself into the coastline to form a fantastic lush glove on the island, inviting people to sail into the island and enjoy the wonders it offers visitors.

The areas that are most traveled are Waimea, Kapa`a, Po`ipu where you will find most of the traffic of tourists go to enjoy the outdoors activities and the lovely beaches, Princeville is known for its development and great condos and villas, Haena is where you will find plenty of local residential areas but also great vacation rentals and cottages, other islands to discover can be found on http://www.kauaiexplorer.com/. 

There is no lack of fun to be had here, but here are some great tips on how to get there and then get around.

Getting to Kauai

Lihue Airport (LIH) is a small airport that takes incoming flights from all of the other islands - http://hawaii.gov/lih/

This is an American State, if traveling from outside of the USA you will need to bring your passport and another form of identification.
