About Vermont

Vermont is a Beautiful State to visit, during the summer time people flock to the small towns and antique shop, or enjoy the beautiful mountains for trekking in.  Most known for its winter-time activities like Skiing, Ice Fishing, and Ice Skating.  Vermont has become a great holiday destination for families and friends alike.  Locals and international guests arrive in Vermont Each year to the laid back and beautiful locations.

Some of the top locations to visit are: Islands and Farms region and Lake Champlain, Stowe and Smugglers Notch for the Skiing and in the summers Lake Elmore for those who love fishing, The Northeast Kingdom with Caledonia/Essex/and Orleans known for its outdoor activities year round, Lake Champlain Valley with its shops and great local restaurants and culture, Central Vermont and it’s great Route 100 where you can drive for hours just exploring and don’t forget to stop for at Waterbury Train Station for some old fashioned hospitality, Mid Vermont for its’ fantastic history and the beautiful 18th Century buildings found in Middlebury and while in town visit the festival on the Green held every  July, The Crossroads represent the very cultural including the arts/jazz/ and the Paramount Theater built in 1913, Eastern Vermont is famous for its Nature and is home to the Vermont Institute of Natural Science, In the South you’ll find Southern Windsor County “the Birthplace of Vermont” you’ll find lots of great museums and local shops that cater to the history, In the Shriers you’ll find plenty of skiing and you’ll have great times in Manchester or just visit Bennington and the many wonderful local attractions, Southern Vermont offers a big plus having had Brattleboro voted one of the best 100 small towns in America and offers wonderful Artisan goods available for sale.

No matter why you go to Vermont or even if you’re passing through, it’s likely to be a State you’ll never forget.
