Cerulean Villa, Barnes Bay, Anguilla


Anguilla Luxury Villa was built to take advantage of its stunning location on Barnes Bay, and the villa is extremely open and airy: with high ceilings, fans throughout, and French doors that open to the sea. The floors are all imported stone tile, and most of the furniture was made specifically for the house by craftsmen in both Mexico and Morocco. The beauty of the house can be attested to, by the fact that it has been photographed by both House and Garden and Italian Architectural Digest.

All of the seven bedrooms are large (an average of 515 square feet) and overlook the sea. Each room has its own private entrance and most have a terrace with a comfortable seating arrangement. Two of the bedrooms have domed ceilings, and they are all air-conditioned with ample closet space. There is a phone in each as well as wireless internet service and cable access for mobile televisions.

The bedrooms have en-suite European style bathrooms that are large and luxurious with imported marble, deck tubs, and glass enclosed showers. Two of the bathrooms also have private outdoor shower gardens.

In addition to the seven bedrooms the 13,000 square feet of living space includes: two living rooms separated by a bar, one indoors with a spectacular vaulted ceiling and mosaics imported from Morocco, the other on a covered terrace with views of the pool and sea below, a dining room that seats 14, a television room that can also convert into an eighth bedroom, a powder room, and a gourmet kitchen.

The house is equipped with cable television (60+ channels), VCRs, DVDs, a stereo system that can connect to guest's iPods, and a library of books, videos, CD’s and games. We have a fax machine for the guest’s use, a cell phone, and a computer with internet connection. We can also provide secretarial services if needed.

Anguilla, one of the most northerly of the Leeward Islands in the British West Indies, offers an enticing combination of attributes for those who truly wish to escape The main island is only sixteen miles long and three miles at its greatest width. With its flat topography, Anguilla doesn’t attract rain clouds the way mountainous islands do. The result is one of the most habitually sunny climates in the entire Caribbean

Add its friendly people and pristine beaches, and you have a favorite vacation destination for discerning travelers who prefer “off the beaten path” privacy; but still enjoy a world-class experience and easy proximity to excellent shopping, invigorating nightlife and exceptional dining options on St. Martin — all a brief crossing away.

Think you can land a wahoo? This is a great place to find out. Feel like a game of golf? You’re in luck. Are you an avid scuba diver? There are over 30 shipwreck sites awaiting your underwater exploration. In recent years, there’s even a burgeoning nightlife with opportunities to dance to reggae under the stars to your heart’s content.
