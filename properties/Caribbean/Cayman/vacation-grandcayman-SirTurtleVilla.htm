Sir Turtle Villa, Little Cayman, Grand Cayman Vacation Rental

 

Sir Turtle Beach Villa defines Caribbean paradise. It sits on an exceptional stretch of private beachfront on the small and quiet island paradise of Little Cayman, with soft white sand to sink your feet into, an endless stretch of azure water to swim, kayak and snorkel, and tilted palms to shade your beachfront nap. Check out the photos—it’s truly postcard-worthy! And 4-bedroom, Sir Turtle Beach Villa #1 provides striking sea views and tons of extras for your beach vacation, including bicycles, kayaks, paddleboards, a boat dock, a fully stocked kitchen, and a whole lot more.

Sir Turtle Beach Villa is a duplex divided into two sides—#1 & #2—and both sides share access to a private boat dock, a sparkling swimming pool at the edge of the Caribbean and a huge front porch looking toward Little Cayman’s wild bird refuge. Inside Villa #1, find a spacious eat-in kitchen with barstool seating and every small and large appliance you need to prepare a seaside feast. A large outdoor gas BBQ provides another cooktop for perfect burgers or grilled fresh fish.

The 4 bedrooms at Sir Turtle Villa (Red 1) include 3 bedrooms with king-sized beds and one with 2 twin beds. A total of 8 guests can sleep very comfortably here.

Little Cayman is an 8-mile long island with 170 full-time residents, a speed limit of 25 miles per hour, and an unmistakable secluded charm. Not to mention some of the best scuba diving in the world! Safely explore Little Cayman and its watery surroundings by bicycle, by kayak, or paddle-board—all of these are provided for you at Sir Turtle Villa at no extra charge! Despite its secluded feeling, Sir Turtle Beach is located within walking distance of two small resorts with restaurants, beach bars, and water-sports shops for the best of both worlds!

NB: There is a hot tub on the outside deck but this is for the Owners use only.

Little Cayman is truly little as the name states! Only ten miles in length and one in width and fewer than 200 people residing there. ‘Little’, as it is affectionately called by locals, is an amazing destination for scuba diving, snorkeling and of course some good old rest and relaxation. Visitors enjoy beach-combing, bird watching, bone-fishing and chilling in a hammock.
