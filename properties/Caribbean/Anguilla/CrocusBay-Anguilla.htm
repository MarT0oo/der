Visiting Anguilla

Known for its amazing beaches, Anguilla is the center of universe from the amazing Beaches on Barnes Bay which are where you’ll find most of the amazing beachfront homes and mansions for rental.  The island itself is only 35 Sq. miles and has a rich history to share with those visiting this island.  The capital, The Valley, has some amazing local shopping and dining, you’ll always find something to do.  Most people do however come for the nautical lifestyle, you can find people swimming, enjoying snorkelling, taking a boat out and traveling to our neighboring islands such as Puerto Rico, St. Martin, and Kitts.  There are so many things to do in the water that people often get submerged for their entire stay.

For those that love to go out at Nightly we have:

     - Elvis Beach Bar

     - Pump House

     - Shoal Bay

     - and so much more. 

For those that like exploring there are Beach Stone Sculptures of Rendezvous Bay, Caves and Holes can be explored with a guide all around the island, You may want to come when sea turtles are hatching to see them run off, you may want to visit the Heritage Collection Museum in the East End Village, or just enjoy the beauty of the famous beaches of Sandy Lane such as Captains Bay, Island Harbour, Shoal Bay East, Limestone Bay, Little Bay, Crocus Bay, Katouche Bay, Road Bay and the village of Sandy Ground, Prickley Pear Cay and Sandy Island, Long Bay, Meads Bay, Barnes Bay, Windward Point, Savannah Bay, Mimi Bay, Sandy Hill Bay, Forest Bay, Little Harbour, Blowing Point, Rendezvous Bay, Merrywing Bay, Cove Bay, Maunday's Bay, and much much more.

Coming to Anguilla is easy, jump on a plane, get on a ferry, or just take a private charter, it’s well worth the trip.
