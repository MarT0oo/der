Concorde Loft, Paris, France Flat Vacation Rental

 

Located on the famous Rue Cambon , just off Rue de Rivoli you are surrounded by prestigious Parisian landmarks in this property, including the Jardins de Tuileries, Place de la Concorde, Place Vendome and of course the infamous Louvre museum.

This property is a traditional Parisian one bedroom apartment situated on the same road as the celebrated Ritz hotel and the famous Fashion House Chanel where Coco herself used to live and create. On entering this fourth floor property you are greeted with a large living and kitchen area in this beautiful loft-style apartment. The spacious living room has retained it’s beautiful and original wooden beams which add to its quintessentially Parisian character. The kitchen is fully-equipped and contains a large dining table. The living room has two large sofas, one of which is a sofa bed and allows this large one bedroom apartment to comfortably cater for up to four people. There is also a separate double bedroom with a large double bed and plenty of storage room. The bathroom contains a shower, toilet, sink and washing machine.

An apartment in a location as esteemed, glamorous and spectacular as this are few and far between in Paris, and this lovely one bedroom apartment is an ideal backdrop to the dream Parisian holiday.

The 1st arrondissement stretches across the rive droite (right bank) of the Seine and is today home to the Louvre museum, Hermès' flagship store and that magnet for the film, fashion and media jet set – the Hotel Costes. The Avenue de L'Opéra is one of Haussman's most successful creations, sweeping all the way from the Louvre northwest to the Palais Garnier. The street is famous for having been the first to be lit by electric arc lamps – for the Paris Exposition of 1878. His elegant buildings with stone facades, intricate wrought-iron balustrades and enormous entrance doors are now synonymous with the look and feel of Paris and this quartier's combination of museums, antique shops, luxury stores all played out amidst Haussmanian architecture is for many the quintessential city experience.
