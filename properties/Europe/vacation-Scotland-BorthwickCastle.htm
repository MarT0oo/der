Borthwick Castle, North Middleton, Gorebridge, Scotland Vacation Rental

 

Built in 1430, Borthwick Castle is located in a pastoral valley, just 12 miles from Edinburgh. Once the refuge of Mary Queen of Scots and the Earl of Bothwell and besieged by Oliver Cromwell in 1650, its Medieval ambiance has been retained to this very day. Guests can enjoy one of 10 unique luxurious bedrooms in the castle and 2 rooms within the castle grounds. The castle's Great Hall, with large fireplace at one end, offers a superb setting for dinner.

We offer an enchanting Scottish castle experience with all the comfort of a beautiful private home, with opulent bedchambers and bathrooms, magnificent yet intimate dining and lounge areas, roaring fires, breath-taking views and spiral staircases inviting exploration. A perfect setting for adventures or relaxation, for young and old, and for creating special memories that will last a lifetime.

There are 10 uniquely designed bedchambers with lavish bathrooms, magnificent yet intimate dining and lounge areas, roaring fires, breath-taking views and spiral staircases, inviting exploration and adventure. Each of the castle's unique bedchambers has been exquisitely refurbished, designed to show off the historic detail, but ensuring the most luxurious experience for guests. The rooms are scattered throughout the castle and mostly are named after the historic figures with connections to the property.

EVERY ROOM TELLS A STORY

The rooms are scattered throughout the Castle, mostly accessed by spiral staircases and secret doorways. Some are named after the historic figures who would have slept there in days gone by. All rooms boast breath-taking views of the beautiful countryside that surrounds the Castle. Modern appliances are encased within solid oak furniture from the Renaissance period.
