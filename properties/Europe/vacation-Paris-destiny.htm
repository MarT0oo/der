Rue Cler Bijou, 7th district, Paris, France Vacation Flat Rental


What could be more romantic than this luscious home made for two (but can stretch to more if you can bear to share it)? Nestled on the third floor above the red and rust canopies of the street stalls below, there is light, polished wood floors, a sophisticated muted palate and exquisite furnishings. The perfectly fitted kitchen has everything you need including a sweet painted table for breakfast; the bathroom a classic clawfoot slipper bath with a view; the bedroom a large comfortable bed. The airy open-plan living area offers a sofa each to loll and plan the day ahead.

In the 7th arrondissement, just a few blocks away from the Eiffel Tower, stroll along rue Cler and you are transported to the Paris of movies, of Charles Trenet's songs and Audrey Hepburn and Fred Astaire dancing under the stars. In this picturesque cobblestoned street, pretty street stalls jostle with neighbourhood cafés, bakeries and flower shops in a cityscape that hasn't changed in a century. Pick up a filled baguette sandwich and a bottle of wine and head off to nearby Champs de Mars for a picnic under the arching legs of Gustav's great tower; nip out in the morning for freshly-baked croissants and a bunch of flowers.

The classy 7th has a little of the feel of Manhattan's Upper East Side. It is home to the French National Assembly and many of Paris' government departments – and the mandarins who work there. Here on the left bank, you also have the Musée d'Orsay and Musée Rodin and a slew of expansive parks and green spaces. Nothing quite beats going for a jog in the early sunshine round the Champs de Mars before taking breakfast up to your own apartment in the heart of the city.
