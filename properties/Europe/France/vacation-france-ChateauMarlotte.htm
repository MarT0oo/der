Chateau Marlotte, Outside Paris, France Vacation Rental

 

Stay in a castle close to Paris

The Château Marlotte offers use of the entire Castle or the rooms, annually the let the Castle for Weddings and Events as well as for family vacations. This luxury Chateaux Hotel is opened all year round in this elegant, family-owned castle Within close vicinity of Paris and 7 kilometers from Fontainebleau, this magnificent and elegant private property was built in the early XVIIth century on the location of a feudal fortress. It is a true architectural example of traditional brick and stone style, and flanked by two small pavilions. Nestled in a private 40 hectare estate, it is surrounded by spring-fed moats. Experience the magic of a stay in this charming French historically classified monument.

The Chateau Features 10 Rooms of varying quality, each with a very historical Feeling. The rooms are serviced and for a small fee ranging from 16 Euros per. person daily you can enjoy a wonderful breakfast at the Chateau. The bedrooms are equipped with all modern conveniences; they also have their charm of yore. Bedrooms are arranged like small private flats and have been completely restored with taste, old furniture, tapestries of Pierre Frey, Brunschwig & Fils, Dédard, Lelièvr. Old wainscots, canopies, recesses, 100% cotton linen. Comfort: ADSL access, Wi-Fi, refrigerator, safe, phone, hair dryer. Air-conditioning in the two rooms on the second floor.

A Castle influenced by the medieval era, during the medieval era, the Castle was a fortress “closed with walls and surrounded by moats with water”. The current map of the Castle is the one determined by the location of the moats. West and east pavilions are posted at the former guardhouses’ places. The huge aspect of the castle’s foundations is built up on the basis of the last fortress. There is uncertainty regarding the precise date of the first building’s construction. However, given some architectural elements, we believe that they were built at the end of the sixteenth century. Windows are projected towards the outside in an awkward, or archaic, way that leaves empty spaces between them.

The 1555 Court of Common in Fleury-en-Bière directly inspires the geometry of the interlocking panels on the low pavilions. Moreover, some other castles were built in the same era around Paris namely; Pontchartrain, Neuville, the Cour des Offices de Fontainebleau.
