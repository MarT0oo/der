Quai Branly Art Déco, 7th district, Paris, France Vacation Rental

 

Located in one of the most beautiful yet peaceful areas of central Paris, the Quay Branly Art Deco shall provide you a modern, creative sanctuary after a busy day of gallery trips and museum tours. Entering the apartment you shall be greeted with a stunning and hugely spacious integrated living, dining and kitchen area. Complete with all mod cons including an espresso machine so that you can enjoy your ritual morning caffeine fix whilst lounging in your Parisian home away from home. Quay Branly Art Deco comes with 3 bedrooms of varying sizes; the master bedroom is equipped with a Kings Size bed and an ensuite which boasts a large shower and bath, a perfect place to relax and prepare for a night of fine dining. The remaining rooms host a queen size bed and 2 twin beds respectively. The latter of which would be perfect for small children with an upper tier storage den, making sure they can keep themselves occupied in their new found room. Overall this apartment is ideal for families or groups looking for a spacious yet creative place in one of Paris’ central districts.

Located on Left Bank, or as a Parisian would say the ‘Rive Gauche’, the 7th arrondissement is set in the heart of Paris. Many of Paris’ most beautiful landmarks, such as the Eiffel Tower, the Ecole Militaire, the Musee d’Orsay and the Hotel des Invalides call the 7th home but do not forget to explore the neighbourhood itself, as it is one of the most beautiful areas in Paris. It is a popular residential area with high-ranking officials and as a result has an upmarket, restrained atmosphere a little like London's Westminster or the Upper East Side in New York. Here you can jog in the Champs de Mars, enjoy art and lunch in the stunning Musee du Quai Branly and stroll around the wide, tree-lined streets feeling like a true, well-heeled Parisian. The 7th arrondissement has it all: beautiful landmarks, comfortable neighbourhood restaurants, bakeries, corner cafés and charming street markets.

You wouldn’t have a truly Parisian experience if you didn’t go to some amazing food markets. A few blocks away, you will find a cute market street filled with little food stalls selling fresh produce - Rue Cler . Just next to Rue Cler, you will find Marie-Anne Cantin - one of the very best cheese shops in the world.
