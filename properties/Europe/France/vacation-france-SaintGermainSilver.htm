This stunning apartment on the fourth floor of a traditional stone building looks as though it has come straight off the pages of an interior design magazine. Every detail of this beautifully-finished bijou property reflects the highest standards of contemporary design from the limed floor to glossy white kitchen. The open plan living area includes a small but fully-equipped kitchen, dining area and beyond a sofa and armchair are arranged around the embedded flatscreen TV.

A sculptural metal staircase with open treads divides the room artistically and leads up to two double bedrooms romantically set under the eaves, each served by a beautifully presented shower room and a separate WC. This is a very grown-up apartment in one of Paris' most desirable locations, served by an elevator with rooftop views of the city skyline.

If you had to choose the most classically Parisian neighborhood in Paris, it would be the 6th arrondissement. The 6th is home to the atmospheric Saint-Germain-des-Prés quartier.

Saint Germain is a myth considered by Parisians and visitors alike to be the quintessential Paris neighborhood. It is the place to see and be seen: Saint-Germain-des-Prés is the Fifties, it’s jazz music, it’s the intellectual Paris where Albert Camus, Jean-Paul Sartre, and Simone de Beauvoir – French philosophers that puffed away on cigarettes while discussing the meaninglessness of existence at the Café de Flore. This area has it all: charming streets, lined with quaint shops, patisseries, bakeries and storied cafés. The area is also an artistic hub, with some of the city’s most prestigious art galleries, designer boutiques, antique shops and a fine selection of museums.
