The clean and modern lines of the villa’s exterior hold five double bedrooms which can comfortably accommodate up to ten guests. The soothing, neutral color palette of the interiors reminds you to indulge in the views of the graceful Zaton bay cloaked in dramatic karst hills. An abundance of cozy details scattered around makes every moment a chance for relaxation.

Upon setting foot in Villa Dubrovnik Earth, you will immediately know that it was designed by a person and for a person. From the stand-out pool with Jacuzzi to the delicate scents of lavender, between the inviting common areas and the comfortable private spaces, Owners vision leads straight to the idyllic unwinding in a beautiful corner of the Earth.

A Walkthrough Villa Dubrovnik Earth: Villa spreads over 350m² of indoors and 120m² of surrounding outdoors. The modern residence crowns the property that terraces down to the road where a garage with two parking spaces is an added bonus if you are travelling by car.

Fronting the house, the expansive terrace lined in stone is this villa’s definite drawcard. You’re likely to spend much of your time here, whether sipping wine in the lovely lounge area or dipping in the 8x3.5m pool with Jacuzzi. With direct views of the bay below, the terrace is the heart and soul of the property.

Backing it, the ground floor of the residence feels like its natural extension. The open-concept common area comprises a fully-equipped kitchen, dining and living area. High ceilings and large glass sliding doors make these spaces wonderfully bright and airy and their light color palette soothes to the senses, accentuated by the back wall of lush greens and a ceiling painted like the sky. Fix up a snack, than sit back in leather armchairs to watch a feisty game of soccer on the immensely large LCD screen in the living area. Cooking and eating together is a very local thing to do, so to the side of the living room, a shaded terrace features an open kitchen with a fireplace, outdoor stove and lavatory, and a dining table for 10. Another large screen TV here ensures that nothing important goes missed. Facing a wall of carob and olive trees, the quality time on this terrace is to be enjoyed in complete privacy.

Villa Dubrovnik Earth has five bedrooms. One sits behind a discreet passage which backs the kitchen and conceals a small service area with a laundry room and a guest toilette. The other four bedrooms are on the upper floor. All bedrooms share a comfortable design, have an en suite bathroom with a walk-in shower and a private balcony. With large closets, vanity tables and all the amenities you might need, they easily grow on you like a second home.

Overall, Villa Dubrovnik Earth feels remote, just perfect for unwinding and focusing on yourself and your loved ones. The over a thousand year old history of Dubrovnik is visible in every part of this city. The city is a living museum and a live stage, and has an ideal connection between its historical past and the modern day. It is surrounded by medieval walls that are 1940 metres long and are preserved in their original form. They are open to visitors and are the city’s greatest attraction. Since 1979 the town has been under UNSECO protection. Dubrovnik is mainly a cultural destination, which asides from monuments of interest, offers a series of cultural events and festivals. Dubrovnik is a destination where you can enjoy a rest, and has extremely good air connections with all the larger European centres. Dubrovnik is a city that charms, a city that you fall in love with and always return to like new, to discover more unique experiences. The historical city of Dubrovnik, the former center of the Dubrovnik Republic, is surrounded by city walls 2 km long which defended the city against attack both on land and at sea for centuries.

Within the city walls a beautiful city developed with many churches, palaces and cultural monuments as well as the Stradun, a well known boardwalk for both young and old from all over the world which was once the stage for all major events. Many islands close by, a crystal blue sea, rich natural and cultural heritage, the Mljet National Park, historical villas and promenades are what make the Dubrovnik Riviera one of the most attractive destinations in Croatia. Here one can enjoy various activities, enjoy autochthonic food and wine and the hospitality of the people. The whole Dubrovnik region is well-known for its fascinating landscapes, abundant Mediterranean vegetation, mild climate and interesting smaller settlements. In former times, it became part of the Dubrovnik Republic territory, becoming connected with the city via historical, economic and trade route ties. In tourism, it is now called the Dubrovnik Riviera.
