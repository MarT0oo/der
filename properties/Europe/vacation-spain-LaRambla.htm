Location

To some, Barcelona is the ultimate city of ‘cool’, from its clothes to its music to its art, where life centres on the tapas bars and cafés, restaurants and clubs; others find its charm in the Old Town with its ancient buildings, narrow streets, Roman ruins and many churches. But, to many, the most striking feature of Barcelona is its architecture, in particular the astonishing works of Gaudi. Above all, perhaps, it is a city of fun and a great place to have a base.

1. Ramble down colourful La Rambla

One of the most famous boulevards in the world, La Rambla is worth a stroll down even if you only have one day in Barcelona. A gateway to rural Catalonia, the mile-long road bustles with tourists, artists, human statues, fortune-tellers, dancers and musicians. Vibrant flower stalls, a cultural and exhibition centre, the superb La Boqueria market, a Joan Miró mosaic, newspaper kiosks and cafés line the street. You may pay a fortune to sip a cola at a roadside café but the people-watching opportunities will be worth the price.

2. Get up close to Gaudí's grand designs

In Barcelona, you can gaze in wonder at Gaudí's fairytale architecture. The Sagrada Familia is breathtaking and grotesque by turns. At first glance, it seems as though a careless giant has dripped melting wax over a Gothic cathedral, but a closer look reveals that the protuberances create a stone tapestry of Christ's life. Take the lift to the top for a breathtaking view. Park Güell.
