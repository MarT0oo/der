Cala Estate Home, Mallorca, Spain Vacation Rental

 

Approached up a long pine tree lined driveway this spectacular two storey villa has a large ground floor terrace and an upper terrace following the length of the villa.

The villa is very light and airy with the surrounding views enjoyable from the many large windows. The lower terrace has an antique glass topped table and dining chairs to one end and a bamboo settee and comfortable armchairs at the other.

To the right as you approach the villa is the walled pool area with a terrace, pool side shower and sun beds. Three steps lead up to a pathway that goes around three sides of the property and leads to two small bridges over a stream and a short garden walk. Trees have been planted to the front aspect of the property facing the road.

The L-shaped kitchen is complete with a breackfast table and chairs and leads out to the pool area. The kitchen and chairs and leads out to the pool area. The kitchen leads to a dining room which has a stone fireplace and may Mallorcan features - both modern and antique. The lounge also features a stone fireplace and comfortable Mallorcan furnishings. Three double glass doors lead out onto the lower terrace. Off the lounge is a twin bedroom located at the rear of the villa.

From the hallway one step up leads to a cloackroom. A large piece of local rock is incorporated into the wall of this room. The staircase leads up to the master bedroom which can be either a double or a twin. This suite is approached through a doorway into a small hall with fitted wardrobes. Off this hall is an en-suite walk-in shower room and doors leading out to the upper terrace. A further double bedroom and a twin bedroom with an en suite bathroom both have doors leading to the upper terrace. A further separate shower room is also on this level.

Majorca is the largest of the four Balearic Islands; in fact its name means “the larger one”. Mallorca, like all of the Balearics, has many aspects to it which make it a wonderful holiday destination. Mallorca is often described as an island of contrasts; it certainly has something to interest many different types of visitors.
