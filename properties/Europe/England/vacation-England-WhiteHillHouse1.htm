White Hill House 1, London, England Vacation Rental

 

A great choice for travelers interested in tourist attractions, convenient public transportation, and beautiful architecture. Inside, guests will find a well accommodated one bedroom apartment with modern décor featured throughout. Comfortable and spacious for up to 4 guests. Located only two minutes from Clapham South Underground station, this apartment offers accommodation with excellent transport links to the rest of London.

A 4 minute stroll to the Clapham South Tube station, in Travel Zone 2, on the Northern Line and can access Central London in about 20 minutes. Popular locations: 2.1 miles from Brixton Academy, 3.9 miles from Stamford Bridge Stadium and 3.9 miles from Natural History Museum London. Victoria and Albert Museum is 4 miles from the apartment, while The All England Lawn Tennis Club Centre Court is 5 miles away. The nearest airport is London City Airport, 12 miles from White Hill House.

This recently renovated Victorian building of five apartments. Features central heating, cable TV with on-demand movies, pay TV, a personal computer and high ceiling for a comfortable stay in London. Guests can take advantage of a bathtub and a shower provided in the bathroom. The large open-plan living area has dining for four, a double sofa bed, and storage space. The bathroom has a bath with a thermostatic fitted shower over it. The kitchen is well equipped, has plenty of storage and a breakfast bar.

Clapham is one of London’s best known and popular historic “villages”. Situated in South West London, it benefits from plenty of greenery, and great transport links to both central London and tourist attractions outside London. The center can be reached in as little as 15 minutes. The tube (northern line) is a two minute walk and there are two supermarkets within a minute of the door.

The area is popular with young affluent families and professionals for its laid-back, friendly, unfussy vibe and open green spaces. Central to this is Clapham Common. This large parkland area, with ponds, cafes and a traditional bandstand (playing concerts in the summer months) is very popular with locals and visitors alike. Even Benjamin Franklin carried out experiments on the pond here. There are numerous traditional, trendy and quirky pubs, all selling good, local craft beers and great food. There’s also a huge variety of restaurants offering cuisine from all around the world. We especially recommend the quaint Clapham Picture House to catch a movie, Venn Street Market every Saturday with its various Street Food stalls, and a walk across the Common to The Old Town to explore the pubs and restaurants there.

Clapham offers you the opportunity to have a local, village-like experience, away from the hordes of tourists.
