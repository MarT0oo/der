Domus Domitia Villa, Rome, Italy Vacation Rental

 

In a seventeenth-century palace building overlooking the tiny beautiful street, Vicolo Domizio in the heart of Renaissance Rome between Piazza Navona and Saint Peters , Domus Domitia welcomes guests in a unique setting.

The entrance is embrassed by the portal of famous Florentine architect Antonio Sangallo, who also participated to the project of Saint Peters with Bramante , among the most famous italian artists of renaissance period.

As you enter Domus Domitia you feel the warm liason between history and modern architecture all around you. The attention to details is stunning. Every element of the house is elegant and appropriate , the structure has been studied thinking of space , light and comfort as a priority , including the former warehouse of the Palace on the lower level . Transaparencies and reflections between the two levels of the residence make it all so special.The elements of interior design are a very eclectic mix , the contemporary art and the traditional furniture emphasizes different Epochs.

The contrast is perfect for those who like history in a modern comfortable and luxurious setting , a true authentic experience in the heart of Roman lifestyle in a very special home .

The independant entrance makes Domus Domitia even more private and reserved although so central. The silent little alley just off famous antique street Via dei Coronari is so charming.

The total white of the Palace is interrupted by shades of green and plum of the sofas from the early twenties , the colours make the living room so lively and pleasant , cozy to enjoy relaxing moments.

The “leit motiv” is remarqued by words and meanings recalled in all of the modern art works in several rooms of the house , in the living room the panel on the wall “ DOMUS DOMITIA” is an inspiration from the night clubs of the 1980’s.

In the first bedroom you are attracted by a panel that lights up , commisionned by a Russian artist who recalls Majakowski with letters expressed in freedom from the soundtrack of Cinderella “ A dream is a wish your heart makes”.

Fabric lamps with futuristic inspiration are creations of the artist Paola Napoleone. From the master bedroom you access to an intimate courtyard with climbing plants , highlight is a roman fountain recuperated from the excavation during work of restoration. Bathroom en suite .

The second bedroom , very cozy has a beautiful headboard in Toile de Jouy designs in delicate shades of grey. Bathroom en suite.

Going down the crystal stairs on the right you can admire a relaxing area of wellness, comfortable armchairs covered in terry cloth are perfect for relaxing after a hammam or hydromassage in the spa tub.

Although the space is very well divided the feeling is of a large loft , next to the wellness area on the left a very spacy and large dining room is dominated by a sign on the wall , words in italic letters a bar sign from the 1950’s invite guests to enjoy dining time.

An open passage leads to the little study where a comfortable sofa bed can accomodate two extra guests . The modern style is underlined by the plexiglass frames around ancient designs of a theatrical costume artist from the 19th century.

From this room you can access to a second courtyard where it is possible to have a drink and relax outdoors.

Through two glass double doors you access to the large and modern kitchen , functional and very well equipped. Inspiring for lovely meals of mediterranean diet or many others the food is easy to find at the fresh outdoor markets very tipical in Rome.

Our desire is to take care of you from the first moment you arrive to the Central Train station or Airport , a car service can be available through Domus Domitia, to take you directly to your "home". At reservation just give us your travel details, and book the transfer!

At arrival you will receive a welcome cocktail by our trusted butler “ jack “ while the manager takes care of your check in documents.

As you enjoy Rome and come back to the house , our Butler “Jack” will be pleased to offer you a coffee , tea or simply a glass of water, he is available in the day until 8pm unless you ask for extra time .
