Villa Crabbia, Pettenasco, Lake Orta, Italy Vacation Rental

 

Villa Crabbia is a beautiful 18th century villa near Pettenasco with a breathtaking view of the romantic Lake Orta.

The villa has been recently and lovingly renovated in its original style and can accommodate a total of 24 guests to a high standard and in modern comfort. This is an ideal location for a large group, a family reunion, business incentive or exclusive wedding by the most romantic lake in all Italy.

This charming villa, built on three floors with a wide panoramic view over Lago d'Orta and Isola San Giulio, accommodates up to 18-24 people in 7 suites/apartments: 2 two-bedroom apartments and 5 one-bedroom apartments. The apartments in total sleep 18 people but it is possible to add beds for up to 24 people (at extra charge). Each apartment comes with a fully-equipped kitchenette, comfortable sitting room and satellite TV.

The spacious communal lounge area (75 m²) with large dining table, stylish furniture, satellite TV, DVD player and wireless internet access is the ideal place for hosting business meetings as well as private parties for family and friends. Degustations of excellent Italian wines can be organised in their private wine cellar.

Outside you'll find a beautiful internal courtyard with fountain and a large terrace with table and deckchairs for you to bask in the sun and be mesmerised by the splendid view over the lake. Surrounding the villa is a large green park (8000 m²) with barbecue facilities and tables.

This large holiday villa is located close to Lago d'Orta (1 km) near the small town of Pettenasco and is only 4 km from the popular tourist resort of Orta San Giulio, where you'll find tempting restaurants and intriguing shops selling traditional Italian handicrafts and food. From the picturesque main square you can hop on one of the many boats to visit the nearby island of Isola San Giulio, or to take a tour and discover the full beauty of the lake. Other fascinating day trips include Lago Maggiore (30 minutes drive), the nearby Alpine mountains, and the famous Piemonte wine region.
