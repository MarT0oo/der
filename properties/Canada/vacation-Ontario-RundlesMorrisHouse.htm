Rundles Morris House, Ontario, Canada Vacation Rental

 

The Rundles Morris House, designed by the Canadian architectural firm Shim-Sutcliffe Architects, is available for rent. Perfect for one couple, two couples, or, a small family, the house functions as a deluxe two-bedroom suite, and has a living room with fireplace and views of the Avon River and surrounding parklands. Throughout the property, one will find only the best in designer modernist furniture and lighting fixtures. Both the master and guest bedrooms feature queen size beds with down comforters and pillows, with the best quality linens sourced from Ireland. Both bedrooms are private, and have bathrooms en suite. The house does not have an elevator, and therefore is not suitable for anyone who cannot climb stairs. Seasonal, healthy gourmet breakfasts are included, as well as daily housekeeping, wireless internet, and complimentary parking for two vehicles on site. (The Rundles Restaurant luncheon or dinner menu may be served in the house during restaurant hours.)

What is special about this house?

“This small Ontario city is home to the Stratford Shakespeare Festival (North America’s largest classical repertory theatre). However, design lovers will want to visit for the Rundles Morris House. A contemporary residence, designed by Shim-Sutcliffe Architects, it’s a sublime combination of materials, in this case concrete, cedar and Douglas fir. The property is spread over four levels, with a central atrium drenching the house in natural light.” [Wallpaper* City Guide Toronto: 2012.]

Rundles Morris House was chosen as a “Record House” by Architectural Record in April 2002. In 2004, the house was chosen for inclusion in The Phaidon Atlas of Contemporary World Architecture, and in the book WhereAbouts: New Architecture with Local Identities.
