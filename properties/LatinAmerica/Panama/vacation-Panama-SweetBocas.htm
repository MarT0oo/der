Bocas del Toro offers a wide range of surf spots from beginner to pro, beach break to reefs and from crowded to empty line-ups. This guide is meant to give you an insight into these waves and when the best conditions are.

The Bocas del Toro Archipelago consists of 9 islands. The islands located on the ocean side receive swells from the Caribbean Sea and all around some of these islands, coral reefs have formed. The swells colliding with these reefs and with some of the sandbanks of the beaches, create beautiful, surfable waves.

Getting There:

VIA PRIVATE AIRCRAFT

Bocas del Toro International Airport (IATA: BOC, ICAO: MPBO) has a single runway with no taxiway. The runway measures 1,500 m × 26 m (4,921 ft × 85 ft). A small terminal exists at the east end of the runway. International arrivals must clear Panamanian customs at the airport. We take care of all paper work for you prior to your arrival. If you are flying private and wish to land in Panama City, you can park your aircraft in our hangar and refuel there. We have an FBO that can fly you round trip PTY/Bocas. Please ask for our information regarding the types of aircrafts available and prices. Flying time is approx. 1 hour.

VIA COMMERCIAL AIRCRAFT

Once in Panama City, your domestic flight leaves from Marcos A. Gelabert airport (PAC) in Albrook, which is about 30 minutes away from Tocumen International Airport (PTY) and only 10 minutes from downtown Panama City. Air Panama (www.flyairpanama.com) flies to Bocas International Airport (BOC) on Isla Colon in the Bocas del Toro Archipelago several times daily. Nature Air flies from several locations in Costa Rica to Bocas del Toro usually twice a week. Either flights are approximately 50 min.
