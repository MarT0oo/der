Langosta surroundings: Tamarindo is the premier beach community in Costa Rica’s Northwest province – Guanacaste. Centered with a stunning one and a half mile crescent beach, Tamarindo is usually the first stop on many visitors itineraries and with good reason. It’s a real town with a dynamic international community of artists, writers, photographers, travelers, surfers, and sun-worshipers. It’s a vibrant beach community with fifty- eight restaurants, nightclubs, hotels, shops, yoga retreats, galleries, spas and a full assortment of recreational opportunities.

Tamarindo is a bustling, cosmopolitan Costa Rican town that contains a mix of Costa Ricans, Europeans and Americans. It is the perfect place to make contact with the heart and soul of Costa Rica that makes living here so worthwhile. And it’s easy to get to - just forty minutes by flight from San Jose or seventy minutes by car from the Liberia International Airport

Tamarindo beach: In recent years the village has become one of the most popular seaside destinations of the country and has experienced growth and development. Therefore, the village offers the accommodations you need to enjoy unforgettable holidays such us a domestic Airport, a variety of boutiques, restaurants and bars, as well as bakeries, supermarkets, car rentals, banks, excellent doctors and pharmacies, two Spanish schools, real states, beauty salons, gym, etc.

Due to the fact that Tamarindo Bay has some of the most tranquil currents of the Pacific Coast of Costa Rica and therefore considered as one of the safest beaches, it is ideal for swimming and other water sport activities. The place is excellent for kayaking, surfing - or boogie-boarding, snorkeling and diving. Sailboat and catamaran trips are also available. They offer special Sunset-Trips in which one can often enjoy spectacular and famous sunsets of this bay. The surrounding areas of the village are great for bicycling and horseback

from the garden: Relaxing on sun chairs in our garden or in the sofa in the patio you can observe the magnificent beauty of Costa Rica’s nature. Birds, howler monkeys, squirrels, gray iguanas, geckos, armadillos, skunks and raccoons are some of the animals that can be seen almost daily throughout the house garden.
