Casa Querencia, Kupuri, Punta Mita, Mexico Vacation Rental

 

Casa Querencia welcomes you from the moment you arrive. Designed by architect Jaime Barba, known for some of the most beautiful homes in coastal Mexico, it offers dramatic water views and cool ocean breezes from every room.

One of the only homes for rent in Kupuri—Punta Mita’s newest, most exclusive enclave—Casa Querencia sits atop a hillside/beachfront double lot, with pools and cascading private gardens all leading down to the undiscovered, soft white sands of Kupuri Beach.

Warm, melon-colored walls, colorful fabrics and traditional Mexican artwork create a vibrant, yet relaxing ambiance. Furnishings have been handcrafted by local artisans using native tropical hardwoods. Lines blur between indoors and out, with the main palapa open on one side to the deep blue Pacific, and on the other to a lush palm garden with a soothing stone water fountain, and flanked by tropical pathways.

Private spaces beckon—a Tuscan-style pizza oven patio shaded by orange trees; a colorful sun terrace with a daybed nook perfect for midday napping or sleeping under the stars; a giant marble outdoor tub set in a private garden.

Perfect for multiple families or large groups, Casa Querencia accommodates 20+ guests. Each of the five air-conditioned suites offer private terraces facing the ocean, and are furnished with comfortable daybeds so guests can read or nap while others swim and play nearby. Bedrooms feature flat-screen TVs, iPod docks, and are appointed with authentic Mexican artwork and traditional furnishings in artful patterns and palettes.

The master suite boasts 180-degree views from a wraparound terrace, an exquisite master bath that includes both indoor and outdoor showers, and a small office located just off the formal entryway.

Two other guest suites feature king-size beds, dramatic ocean views and large, luxurious baths. A fourth suite overlooking the pools has two queen beds, and a bunk room just off the pools sleeps six, with indoor and outdoor showers.

In addition, a loft above the media room sleeps four on pullout futons, while the media room couches can accommodate additional overflow guests.

One of the things that sets Casa Querencia apart is our people. The full-time, professional staff is encouraged to be perceptive about each guest’s personal preferences, making it easy for you to relax and enjoy your vacation. We want you to feel at home at Casa Querencia, and our staff makes that their first priority.

Our concierge will make all your arrangements ahead of your arrival, coordinating arrival times and food preferences. She’ll also arrange restaurant reservations, whale-watching, zip line adventures, surfing lessons, or whatever else you may desire.

Our driver will collect you at the airport and shuttle you to the home, where our butler will serve you a margarita as soon as you arrive. Guests typically rave about our chef, who prepares a variety of delicious, traditional Mexican dishes and local specialties using the freshest seafood, fruits and vegetables.

Guests can’t seem to say enough about our staff, who work overtime to make sure your stay is truly relaxing and memorable.

Quickly becoming the destination of choice for discerning travelers, Punta Mita is on a point at the northern tip of the Bay of Banderas, the second-largest bay in the Americas. The securely-gated community is anchored by the Four Seasons and St. Regis hotels and two championship Jack Nicklaus golf courses, all nestled between the majesty of the Sierra Madre Mountains and nine miles of pristine beaches and the turquoise waters of the Pacific.

Punta Mita is blessed with an almost ideal climate; temperatures range from the 70s in the winter to the 80s in the summer, with cooling sea breezes year-round.

Kupuri is considered to be among the finest residential communities within the Punta Mita Resort. Casa Querencia sits atop a hillside/beachfront double lot, with pools and cascading private gardens all leading down to the undiscovered, soft white sands of Kupuri Beach.

For centuries the Huichol Indians, of Mexico’s Sierra Madre Mountains, have believed that nature and the inner spirit are forever intertwined as a life force known as “kupuri.” With your first glimpse of the gently sweeping oceanfront landscape that is the setting for Casa Querencia, you’ll instantly understand why.
